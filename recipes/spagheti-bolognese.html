Description

Spaghetti Bolognese is a popular pasta dish outside of Italy, though it is not considered part of traditional Italian cuisine. The dish typically consists of spaghetti served with a meat-based sauce, often referred to as Bolognese sauce. However, the sauce used in Spaghetti Bolognese often bears little resemblance to the authentic Italian rag√π alla bolognese, which is traditionally served with tagliatelle rather than spaghetti.

The origins of Spaghetti Bolognese are unclear, but it may have evolved in the context of early 20th-century emigration of southern Italians to the Americas or developed in immigrant restaurants in Britain in the post-war era.

Ingredients

  • 3 slices bacon, diced
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 1 carrot, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 400g lean ground beef
  • 2 cans crushed tomatoes 800g
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh basil
  • 1 package spaghetti 500g
  • Parmesan cheese

Steps

  1. Fill a large pot with lightly salted water and bring to a rolling boil. Stir in spaghetti and return to a boil. Cook spaghetti uncovered, stirring occasionally, until tender yet firm to the bite, about 12 minutes; drain.
  2. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat; add bacon. Cook, turning occasionally, until crisp, 8 to 10 minutes.
  3. Add onion, carrot, and oregano to bacon; continue cooking until vegetables begin to soften, 8 to 10 minutes. Add garlic; cook until fragrant, about 2 minutes.
  4. Crumble in ground beef; cook and stir until no longer pink and completely cooked, 8 to 10 minutes.
  5. Stir in crushed tomatoes, basil, bring to a boil, season with salt and black pepper, then remove from heat.
  6. Spoon sauce over spaghetti; top with Parmesan cheese to serve.
